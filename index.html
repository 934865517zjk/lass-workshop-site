The 1st Workshop on LASS: LLM Agent for Social Simulation

Social simulation has long played a crucial role in exploring the mechanisms underlying human behavior and societal structures. Traditional social simulation relies on rule-based or statistical models, which makes it difficult to capture the complexity and variability of the real world. With the emergence and rapid development of large language model (LLM), new frontiers have been opened toward leveraging LLMs as agent to model human behavior and interactions. This cutting-edge direction has gained significant attention and demonstrated promising results, not only advancing research across a wide range of social science disciplines, but also enabling practical applications in role-playing scenarios. However, this field still faces multiple challenges, such as capturing real-world social phenomena, eliminating bias or ethical considerations, and ensuring usability and reliability. This workshop on LLM Agent for Social Simulation aims to bring together researchers and practitioners from diverse backgrounds to foster interdisciplinary collaboration, address key challenges, explore cutting-edge technologies, and chart promising future directions in this rapidly evolving field.

Call for Papers

We welcome submissions focusing on: \vspace{-2mm} \begin{itemize}[label=$\bullet$] %[leftmargin=*] \item \textbf{Validation of Social Phenomenon} \begin{itemize}[label=$*$] \item \textbf{Micro-level}: individual behaviors including event evolution, interaction patterns and mechanisms that shape interpersonal dynamics, etc. \item \textbf{Macro-level}: large-scale patterns including societal trends and emergent group behaviors that characterize collective social phenomena, etc. \end{itemize} \item \textbf{Alignment with Social Value} \begin{itemize}[label=$*$] \item \textbf{Environment Construction}: Text, virtual, embodied, robotics, etc. \item \textbf{Agent Architecture}: Mechanism or module such as memory, perception, preference, reflexion, action, etc. \item \textbf{Alignment and Personalization}: Reinforcement learning from human feedback, offline preference optimization, in-context learning, representation editing, inference-time alignment, role play, etc. \end{itemize} \item \textbf{Evaluation Methodologies} \begin{itemize}[label=$*$] \item \textbf{Metrics}: Evaluation metrics for emotion, cognition, preferences, social values, and other factors at the individual, group, and societal levels. \item \textbf{Benchmarks}: Standardized testbeds or performance benchmarks, etc. \end{itemize} \item \textbf{System Scalability} \begin{itemize}[label=$*$] \item \textbf{Scale}: Large-scale populations, high-order interactions, etc. \item \textbf{Efficiency}: Agent systems designed for parallel processing, distributed computing, or resource-constrained environments, etc. \item \textbf{Scaling Law}: Scaling properties in multi-agent social simulations, etc. \end{itemize} \item \textbf{Practical Applications} \begin{itemize}[label=$*$] \item \textbf{Toolkit}: Platforms, codebases or software solutions for developing and deploying social simulations with large language models. \item \textbf{Vertical Field}: Applications in specific field such as games and virtual worlds, healthcare and medicine, education, and other domain-specific use cases where social simulations and agent-based models are valuable. \end{itemize} \item \textbf{Ethical Considerations}: Ethical implications, risk, bias and fairness, etc.
